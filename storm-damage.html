How It Works

From Storm to Settled Claim

1. Free Inspection

We walk your roof and document hail hits, wind lift, and granule loss with dated photos.

2. Damage Report

You receive a written report you can hand directly to your insurance carrier when you file.

3. Adjuster Meeting

We meet your insurance adjuster on-site to walk the roof together and point out documented damage.

4. Claim Approved & Scheduled

Once your claim is approved, we schedule the replacement and work directly from the insurance scope.

Why This Matters

Adjusters See Hundreds of Roofs a Season

Signs to Look For

Do You Have Storm Damage?

You may not see it from the driveway. These are common signs worth a free inspection.

Granule Loss

Excess granules in gutters or downspouts after a storm.

Soft or Bruised Spots

Hail impacts that dent the shingle mat without visibly cracking it.

Creased Shingles

Wind lift that creases a shingle without fully detaching it.

Damaged Gutters or Vents

Dented gutters, downspouts, or roof vents are a strong sign hail also hit the roof.

Neighbors Filing Claims

If nearby homes are getting new roofs after a storm, yours is worth checking too.

A Recent Severe Storm

Even if nothing looks obviously wrong, damage can go unnoticed for months and worsen over time.
